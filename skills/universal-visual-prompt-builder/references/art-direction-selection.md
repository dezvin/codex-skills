# Art Direction Selection

## Purpose

Use this reference after the visual goal and dominant meaning carrier are clear
and before writing the production approach. Its job is to select one task-fit
artistic world without making the user perform routine art direction.

Return one final direction, not a style menu. Keep unused candidates and
rankings internal.

## When To Compare Directions

Run a full comparison when the task needs a substantive new visual direction,
the brief is visually open, or the obvious first treatment looks generic.

Constrain or skip the comparison when:

- the user has deliberately locked the style or medium;
- an edit must preserve the working artistic world;
- strict references control the relevant visual decisions;
- the request asks for a narrow correction rather than a new direction.

Do not use exploration as a reason to violate preservation requirements.

## Derive Candidates From The Task

Silently form enough materially distant candidates to expose the real choice.
Do not target a fixed candidate count and do not walk through a mandatory style
menu. Stop expanding when additional candidates would no longer change the
selection.

A candidate is a complete working hypothesis:

- artistic world or production tradition;
- why it makes the task's meaning visible;
- dominant medium and image-making process;
- composition and attention logic;
- relationship between text and image when text exists;
- main execution risk;
- fit with references, format, preservation rules, and hard constraints.

Candidates are materially distant when they differ in how the image is made or
understood. Color variants, camera-angle variants, or several versions of the
same glossy render are not distant candidates.

Derive the candidate space from the visual function, meaning carrier, intended
use, and constraints. If the first candidates cluster around one familiar
solution, deliberately cross into a genuinely different production logic:
photographic, drawn, printed, painted, typographic, spatial, material,
technical, cinematic, screen-native, craft-based, or another task-relevant
tradition. These are escape routes from a local default, not required
categories or reusable presets.

The deliverable label is not an art direction. Words such as `cover`, `post`,
`slide`, `carousel`, or `story` control use, hierarchy, ratio, and text; they do
not automatically require editorial graphics, paper craft, interface language,
or another familiar convention.

## Select One Direction

Select in this order:

1. task function and semantic precision;
2. exact text, format, preservation, and hard-constraint fit;
3. ability to express the chosen medium convincingly;
4. compositional and production coherence;
5. distance from the most obvious stock treatment;
6. distinctiveness.

Keep only the strongest plausible alternatives long enough to challenge the
leader: which direction makes the meaning more immediate, less generic, and
more native to its medium without weakening the constraints?

Distinctiveness never beats task fit. Do not choose a radical direction only
because it is unusual.

If every candidate remains generic, expand once into more distant production
logics. If the result is still weak because the visual goal or meaning carrier
is materially under-specified, ask for that missing decision. Do not ask the
user to choose a style merely because selection is difficult.

## External Discovery

Use bounded external discovery only when a browsing or retrieval tool is
available and one of these conditions holds:

- the promising medium or tradition is unfamiliar or culturally specific;
- internal candidates remain generic after one expansion;
- current or rare visual knowledge materially improves the result;
- the user explicitly requests a source, artist, studio, genre, or model fit.

Start from the task and a plausible direction, not from random gallery
browsing. Retrieve only enough to understand production logic. Extract
principles and craft controls; do not copy an external prompt as the result.

External discovery is optional. When it is unavailable or unnecessary, select
from supported knowledge and proceed honestly.

## Translate Named Sources

An artist, studio, movement, film, game, brand, or era is a compressed
reference, not a complete production approach. Translate it into visible
controls:

- medium and production process;
- form and mark-making;
- space and composition;
- light and color behavior;
- surface, material, and finish;
- typography or text-image relationship when relevant;
- intentional irregularity or precision.

Use one dominant source by default. Add a secondary influence only when it owns
one explicit function and does not compete with the dominant base. Do not stack
names as decorative keywords.

## Failure Checks

Reject or repair the selected direction when:

- style replaces the visual task;
- a famous name replaces concrete craft control;
- several influences compete without hierarchy;
- the direction could illustrate almost any topic unchanged;
- the deliverable label selected the artistic world by habit;
- the medium is described only through empty quality adjectives;
- novelty damages exact text, factual grounding, identity, references, or
  preservation requirements.
